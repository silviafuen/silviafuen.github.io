Lyric Translations

Responsive Design + Prototype

Goal

Develop a better platform to view and learn translated lyrics.

User persona

International music fan, Artists(Singers/Dancers)

Process

I used twitter polls for this project and surveyed my followers with 11 questions, about the current state of lyric translations. From that I gathered that a majority of them viewed them on mobile and that 86% of them wanted a better option. A majority of them as well used Google search results. I used Google's Material framework because users are already accustomed to that view and build out the wireframe from there.

About Me

I'm a 4th-year Computer Science student at Tarleton State University. I love The Simpsons and enjoy bookmarking Medium articles I'll eventually get too. My interest in technology started off after watching a Google documentary in middle school and finding out there were people behind it all. After trying out and studying sectors of the tech industry, I learned that I can make the greatest impact in designing for the users with the skill set I've enjoyably accumulated and my curious mindset.

I have been designing graphics for the past 7 years(a few more if you count my Microsoft Paint days) and since entering the CS program I have evolved from making pretty visuals into using my skills as a tool to find visual solutions and better implement plans. I have published Snapchat geofilters, Onboarding screens, and used my skills to build responsive sites for my University.

I'm currently open to opportunities upon graduating in May.
