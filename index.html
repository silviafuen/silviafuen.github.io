Lyric Translations

Responsive Design + Prototype

Goal

Make viewing lyrics less of a hassle and deliver a better experience for userslearn translated lyrics.

User persona

International music fan, Creatives(Singers/Dancers)

Process

I started survying my followers on twitter using polls asking 11 questions, about what they thought of current lyric translations options. I gathered that a majority of them viewed them on mobile and that 86% of them wanted something better. I also asked them what they used the most, it was typically Google's first result or a Youtube search. From their feedback, their main issue was comparing the languages on mobile. The other issue was formatting and how that complicated their understanding of what they viewed. I worked on taclking those two issues with a mobile version.
I'm currently working on developing this solution into a full site as my senior project. View more about the project »

About Me

I'm a 4th-year Computer Science student at Tarleton State University. I love The Simpsons and enjoy bookmarking Medium articles I'll eventually get too. After trying my hand in various areas of tech, I found myself always going back to design. In focusing on that, I've learned that I can make the most significant impact in designing products for users from my years of working with clients and studying systems.

I have been designing graphics for the past 7 years(a few more if you count my Microsoft Paint days) and since entering the CS program, I have evolved from designing visuals into using my developing skills as a tool to find solutions and better implement ideas through prototyping. I have published Snapchat geofilters, Onboarding screens, and used my skills to build responsive sites for my university.

I'm currently open to opportunities upon graduating in May.
