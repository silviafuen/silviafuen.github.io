Creating an online student community

A university's homepage serves a varied audience, and to alleviate some of that responsibility, Tarleton State University implemented the Current student's page somewhere near 2010. It had high visibility as it served as the default homepage for on-campus desktops, but had little interaction. At the time of its inception, it served its purpose, and students relied on it to get where they needed on the University site along with external services. But with changing device views, new students in different stages of life, and a change in the campus community, it was no longer helping.

My role on this project was to lead design and development after user research was completed. I worked on analyzing studentâ€™s feedback and identifying ways to meet their needs and bring the page up to date, implementing them in varying prototypes, handled information structure, prototyping versions of the page and building it out within our Content Management Server (CMS).

Strategy

Using a combination of student feedback from surveys and viewing analytic reports of the pageâ€™s activity. We were able to observe what was being used, and heard from students what they thought would be the most helpful to them from a homepage. We identified our audience of users to this page, which as a university can vary. Our audience is composed of the following:

  • First time (Traditional) Students ( 18 - 22 yr old students living on or near campus)
  • Distance Learners(Online Students) (20 + Working full time, hours away from campus)
  • Nontraditional Students ( 28+ Returning to university, or entering for the first time)

Keeping these types of student in mind, we had to be aware that each had different goals, could overlap. We continued with a content audit determining which elements serviced these 3 student types the best. We decided to cut and regroup certain sections of the existing page, regrouping, finding a middle ground between information and content.

We came away with a new set of sections for the site and began discussing how we could implement them best with our responsive framework. Working through a cycle of wireframing blocks, prototyping, and rearranging the structure to one that allowed users to move from one section to the next knowing what would be coming.

Solution

We created a space that made students feel welcome and apart of the broader campus community no matter where or who they were. We did this with a weekly blog, that's written by our students covering topics that come with the seasons and how to take advantage of university resources, like an overly helpful friend. We went to this with solutions from a combination of our survey results, brainstorming, and pitching to other student groups.

Keep Up With Campus was another new feature what we came up with to serve one of the most significant needs of all three groups, staying up to date with the campus. Students can learn about what they may have forgotten or not known about the university be it academic, university-wide or social.

Closing
