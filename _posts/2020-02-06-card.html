Changes based on feedback & development

I was leaning more toward the White & Red version, but there were two issues with the design itself.

  1. Iconography alone was unclear to vistors unfamiliar with certain platforms - based on feedback from a twitter follower
  2. In development the link hover, didn't flow as easily as I anticipated
I went back to the Figma Artboard to prototype a few different hover options. I made the switch over to the ring as it had a softer hover reaction and is an addition instead of flash of change, and went for a filled icon because the edges on outlines were too rough.

[link icon mockup vs live]

I chose to have the links accompanied by the platform's name, as it was a more inclusive approach, to unfamilar users and those with assitive tools.

The Outcome

I ultimately went with White & Red, because it was simple and really spoke to my own plain, yet bold style(fonts) I incorporate into my everyday wardrobe and makeup.

I programmed it from scratch over a weekend using a responsive skeleton framework. Used Google Fonts, and Font Awesome for the icons and text.

[webpage screenshot]

Learnings

I launched silviafuen.com about 2 weeks later after I figured out how to use netifly thanks to a friend.

This was probably one of the quickest projects, I've ever worked on. What helped the most was the feedback from the twitter community and just the pure excitement to work on something from end-to-end.
