First Financial Bank

Utilizing personalization to improve the banking experience to fit the customer's lifestyle and needs.

First Financial Bank(FFB) is a small bank system across West and Mid Texas. Each year they are opening more and more branches, mainly near small universities in Texas. As a result, they’ve had an influx of customers that range from 18 - 24yrs.

The Goal

FFB wants to create a better experience for this younger demographic while keeping their existing customer base. They want to improve the experience of their current digital applications to attract and maintain their younger audience.

The Problem

The bank doesn’t know precisely much about their audience and their habits and is not sure what to do to enhance their experience.

Our Strategy

We developed a strategy to tackle this issue and understand where the bank was failing their customers, so we developed the following strategy which at times, overlapped and went back and forth.

  • Learn more about this audience using provided research
  • Audit the existing services that FFB already provides
  • Develop a Persona using the research findings & knowledge of the bank’s services
  • Find areas in the user’s experience where the bank’s services aren’t aligning with their lifestyle
  • Develop solutions that allow the bank to rise to the customer’s needs using existing services and possibly introducing new services.

Research & Persona

Having no prior research conducted or much knowledge aside from the age of our customers we had to find outside resources to give us the knowledge we needed.

Accenture conducted an excellent study titled How Can Banks Meet Customer Demands? Identifying the types of customers a bank has and the expectations of each.

This study helped me develop a persona of the type of customer FFB is trying to reach. With some storyboarding along with the Accenture study I came up with the following persona

Toby, 20yr old college student:
  • Living on his own for the first time in an apartment with 2 friends
  • Wants to watch his spending habits to make sure he has enough to pay his bills and go out on the weekends
  • Needs to access his account quickly in person and online to easily know how much he has when spending
  • Doesn’t have time to go to the bank between his classes and work at the local grocery store

The Audit

In our Services audit, we decided to create a comparison chart displaying what each offered and found that the mobile application was missing a significant number of services that the desktop website offered, and offered additional services/options that served the customer no use. Findings

With our audit results and persona Toby, we began comparing side by side Toby’s needs and lifestyle to what FFB was offering. Here are a few of the major points we found:

  • They were not offering the same services across all devices. The differing offers in service limited the usefulness of having a mobile app and website. For some students and particular demographics computers are hard to reach, so extending the services that are on the web to mobile app would help enhance the experience of customers who only have access to mobile.
  • They are not utilizing the information provided by their customers and providing the personalization they want. So many young adults like Toby, want personalization about their spending habits. 59% want tools helping them monitor their monthly budget, with real-time adjustments based on their spending.
  • They aren’t notifying their user of immediate account changes or deals they may have, and 58% of young users would like banks to send them information about services exactly when needed.

Solutions + Closing

FFB should focus on introducing new services that are personalized to customers like Toby using the information they already have provided. They can personalize offers and help customers create budgets and track spending with such information.

For the critical issues mentioned above, here are a few solutions we’ve come up with for them to better reach customers like Toby.


  • Introduce a Budgeting and spending tracker, that uses a customer’s existing transaction data to build a budget that works for them. Taking inspiration from current mobile budget apps, such as Mint and Quicken, FFB can do something similar. Here’s a quick mockup of what that could look be.

    A spending tracker can help user’s like Toby visual how much and what they spend on. He’ll be able to create a budget that lets him decide how much he needs to pay his bills and still have some left over for a weekend.
  • Make essential services more accessible to access and complete on mobile. Currently, bill pay isn’t available on the Mobile app but is on the Web. This isn’t helpful for those who are always on the move and can’t quickly reach a desktop.
  • Send push notifications and text message updates, based on custom settings for spending. Expanding notifications would be a good solution for Toby who is continually moving, and he may not remember how much he has in his account. Implementing push-notifications can ensure that Toby can be informed at the moment if his account is running low if payment has been made if he’s received his paycheck or is below a certain amount and should stop spending.

If FFB takes the time now to invest in design and tailor the services they offer to fit groups of individual customer’s lifestyle, they'll be able to develop a trusting bond with their audiences. In growing a bond, it’s shown that 59% would like banks to help with purchases like buying a car/house. Moreover, FFB can help there and grow alongside this young generation into adulthood and be trusted to help make more significant decisions such as purchasing a home
