Mango Health App Monitoring Feature

UX, User flows, Prototyping / Spring 2019

Monitoring user's behavior and sharing insights to help them live healthier, better lives.

Overview

As a part of my 2019 KP Design Fellows application, I was asked to Redesign a feature of a KP portfolio company's product.

With interest in healthcare, I chose Mango Health. Mango Health first launched in 2012, helps users manage their medicine and create healthy habits with occasionally opportunities to win rewards for keeping a streak.

I focused on redesigning recording patient's intake through need-finding research.

My role

As an external designer, I audited the existing app's experience, gathered customer and team insight to deliver an experience that aligned with the company's objectives.

Company Objectives

Based on conversations with the Mango Health team, I came away with objectives they wanted to reach moving forward:

  1. Make products that are easy to use, delightful to interact with
  2. Build good habits around daily health regimens
  3. Share insight on user's habits

The Problem

Throughout my need-finding conversations with the team and customer research, there was an underlying themes I noticed, even among the requests from long-time users. I focused on:
How do we make the app smarter for users?

This was a problem that required long term improvements to achieve.

My strategy was to improve an existing feature that aligned with this and lay out the framework to reach our larger goal subsequently.

So I set out to solve the short term problem:

How do we give users a way to track their habits productively?

I focused on redesigning the Intake flow to address this and resolve the user request to record intake early, later or skip.

This flow was already asking for users to input their time of intake but it wasn't doing anything productive with that information.

My Process

I carried out a design-sprint to identify a solution over 3 weeks that consisted of

  • Need-finding conversations with Mango Health Team
  • Customer Feedback reviews
  • Redesigning user flows
  • Solution sketching + Wireframing
  • Mockups & Prototyping in Figma
  • Engineering Feasibility conversations

The Outcome

In redesigning this interaction to instead ask why they took their medication early, late or skipped, allowed us to use that information in a new productive manner.

Using that information to identify patterns in the user's recordings and share that insight with them to encourage a change in their behavior.

This resolved a complaint from users that I identified in research and met the company's main objectives.


Long term solution

The new Recommendations flow, that encourages a user to change their intake time to one that better suits their lifestyle. Making the app smarter for our users.

Retrospective

I designed a feature that made in impact in the present and set us up for the future. This experience opened me up to how to collect data ethically and a new research method by viewing app store reviews.

In that same vein, I wish I could have interviewed long-time users of the app about their experience and the proposed redesign, to fully address any assumptions I had made.

This project allowed me to design with intention and use information to help our users live a healthier life.
